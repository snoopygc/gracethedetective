Cyber-Crime Case-Studies Map

How to Solve a Basic Case: The Pirate's Guide

  1. Gather ye evidence: Collect all related logs and data
  2. Chart yer course: Establish a timeline of events
  3. Identify the scurvy dogs: Look for suspicious activities or users
  4. Follow the treasure trail: Trace the attacker's actions
  5. Secure the booty: Implement fixes and patch vulnerabilities
  6. Share yer tale: Document findings and share lessons learned

1. Petya/NotPetya Cyberattack

A global ransomware attack that caused billions in damages, targeting Ukraine but spreading worldwide.

2. LinkedIn Data Breach

Over 100 million user accounts compromised in 2012, with passwords stolen and later sold on the dark web.

3. Yahoo Data Breach

All 3 billion Yahoo accounts were affected in 2013-2014, one of the largest data breaches in history.

4. SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack

Sophisticated attack compromised software updates, affecting thousands of organizations including US government agencies.

5. Colonial Pipeline Ransomware Attack

Ransomware attack on major US fuel pipeline led to fuel shortages and highlighted infrastructure vulnerabilities.

6. Facebook Data Leak

Personal data of over 500 million Facebook users leaked online, exposing phone numbers and other information.

7. Mirai Botnet Attack

Massive DDoS attack using IoT devices took down major websites and services across the internet.

8. Ashley Madison Data Breach

Hack of infidelity website exposed user data, leading to blackmail attempts and at least two suicides.

9. Twitter Bitcoin Hack

High-profile Twitter accounts compromised to promote Bitcoin scam, revealing vulnerabilities in social media security.

10. Anthem Health Insurance Hack

Major health insurer breach exposed personal information of nearly 80 million customers and employees.

11. Sony Pictures Hack

Devastating cyberattack leaked confidential data, emails, and unreleased films, allegedly by North Korean hackers.

12. Marriott Hotels Data Breach

Long-running breach affected up to 500 million guests, exposing passport numbers and other sensitive data.

13. WannaCry Ransomware Attack

A massive ransomware attack that infected over 200,000 computers worldwide, exploiting a vulnerability in Microsoft Windows, causing widespread disruptions, including in the UKâ€™s NHS.

14. Equifax Data Breach

Hackers stole sensitive personal information, including Social Security numbers, from 147 million people in one of the largest breaches of personal data in U.S. history.

15. Dyn DDoS Attack

A distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack that took down major websites like Twitter, Netflix, and Reddit by targeting Dyn, a DNS service provider, through a botnet of IoT devices.
